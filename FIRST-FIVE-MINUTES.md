\# First Five Minutes



\*A quick introduction to the Continuity Recovery Handshake (CRH)\*



\---



\## Why does CRH exist?



Have you ever returned to an AI conversation and wondered:



\* Does it know where we left off?

\* Can I trust what it says it remembers?

\* What changed while I was away?

\* What's the safest way to continue?



Those are \*\*orientation problems\*\*.



CRH exists to help people and AI become jointly oriented before continuing.



\---



\## What is CRH?



The \*\*Continuity Recovery Handshake (CRH)\*\* is a framework for recovering orientation after interruption, uncertainty, or disruption.



It does \*\*not\*\* assume continuity.



Instead, it encourages both participants to establish:



\* what is known;

\* what is unknown;

\* what evidence has been inspected; and

\* the next safe move.



The goal is not to pretend uncertainty doesn't exist.



The goal is to manage uncertainty honestly.



\---



\## A simple example



Imagine returning to a conversation after your browser crashed.



Instead of saying:



> "Everything is fine. Let's continue."



A CRH-oriented conversation might say:



\* Here's what I know.

\* Here's what I cannot verify.

\* Here's the evidence I've inspected.

\* Here's what remains uncertain.

\* Here's the next safe step.



That gives both participants a shared starting point.



\---



\## What makes CRH different?



CRH does not ask you to trust claims you cannot inspect.



Instead, it encourages observable evidence whenever practical.



Examples include:



\* verifying important documents;

\* checking repository integrity;

\* disclosing uncertainty;

\* distinguishing observations from conclusions.



One of the project's guiding ideas is simple:



> \*\*Inspection before inference.\*\*



\---



\## How did CRH develop?



CRH was not designed around a fixed theory.



It evolved by observing real interactions between a human and an AI across interruptions, device changes, recovery attempts, and verification exercises.



As observations accumulated, the model changed.



One principle gradually emerged:



> \*\*Let the evidence shape the model.\*\*
